📛
Our name is LEE!
In Chinese it is written like this:
李
李 means the plum tree. 🌸
It is one of the most common names in China!
⏰
Our family is VERY old!
How old?
🦕
3,000 years old!
That is older than the dinosaur bones at the museum!
30+ generations of grandparents... then theirs... then theirs... all the way back!
🗺️
We come from China!
A place called Taishan
台山
🏔️
Taishan is next to the big ocean in southern China. Lots of Chinese families in America come from Taishan! 🌊
Long long ago...
👑
We had a KING in our family!
🤴
Emperor Gaozu
He started the Tang Dynasty — one of the greatest empires ever! That is like being the most powerful person on Earth.
618 CE — over 1,400 years ago!
🏛️
The Tang kept growing!
🤴
Emperor Taizong 李世民
Son of the first Tang emperor — he helped turn the dynasty into a golden age of trade and learning. People called him "Heavenly Khan." Our family tree touches that same royal line!
626–649 CE — famous for fair rule and a strong empire. 🌟
⚔️
We had a GENERAL too!
🏹
Li Sheng 李晟
He was a brave soldier who saved the capital city from bad guys! The emperor was SO thankful. He is our direct great-great-great-...-grandpa!
727 CE — over 1,300 years ago!
How we got to Taishan...
⛵
A stormy boat trip!
Our ancestor Wing Choeng (荣昌) sailed on a ship. A huge storm came! 🌊💨
He was very sick. But he pointed his finger to a mountain and said...
"Build our home THERE!" 👆🏔️
That mountain was in Taishan. And that became our family's home for 900 years!
A fun story...
🪙
He found secret treasure!
Our ancestor Siu Yip was being kind...
🌸 He was caring for his dad's grave. He found silver coins in a stream — and started a duck farm!

🛤️ Later he widened a road to help everyone travel. Under the ground was another coffin full of silver coins!

🦆 He used the money wisely. The family did really well!
He was kind FIRST — and found treasure BOTH times! 🌟
A funny story...
🐟
The thriftiest grandpa!
Sing Jom 承淦
He was SO careful with money...
He ate ONE tiny fish 🐟
with THREE mouthfuls of rice 🍚
to make it last!
But because he saved every penny, he was able to buy land for his children. 💚
A loving story...
💌
$2 and a letter
Our ancestor Tin Wai went far away as a teacher and to help sick people get better.
👨‍🏫
Once a year, he sent home...
$2 💵 and a letter 📬
saying he was okay.

The whole family waited all year for that letter! They missed him so much.
🚢
Great-great-grandpa sailed to America!
Yau Jee 祐志
🚢 He sailed on a big ship called the SS Gaelic (it stopped in San Francisco first!)
📅 Year: 1897
📍 He made his home in Chicago!
🎰 He won the lottery and sent $3,000 home to the family!
He ran a laundry — washing clothes for people — and worked very hard! 💪
The most amazing person...
🦸
Meet Lee Yick Poon!
He is like the superhero of our family story!
👴
Lee Yick Poon 奕揖
Born in China in 1891. He did something VERY brave to come to America...
🤫
He wore a disguise!
⚓ He dressed up as a sailor
🚢 He hid on a cargo ship
🌊 He crossed the Pacific Ocean
🇺🇸 He arrived in America!
His own words: "The hardship I went through was indescribable." 😢
Year: 1929
🏙️
He worked in Chicago!
🍽️ He worked in a restaurant
💵 He earned $20 a month
(That is very little money!)
👨‍👦 His dad Yau Jee was in Chicago too!
Father and son — in the same city for 12 years!
He moved to San Francisco in 1942. 🌉
🌉
San Francisco!
He lived at 4 Spofford Alley in Chinatown.
🏠
The building was old and creaky.
His granddaughter Lillian rolled down the stairs at least twice! 😂

People said a ghost lived there... 👻
Lee Yick Poon always wanted to see the ghost. He never did! 👻
🎉
He became American!
📜
February 17, 1965
Lee Yick Poon became a US citizen.
He was 73 years old!
It took his whole life — but he did it! 🇺🇸
📖
He wrote it all down!
In March 1963, Lee Yick Poon wrote a diary so his family would never forget where they came from.
"I have raised my children. My goal has been fulfilled." 💛
His granddaughter Lillian translated the diary. His daughter Olivia did the research. That is how we know this story!
👟
The hungry shoes story!
"Grandpa saw our shoes and said, 'looks like your shoes are getting hungry!
Here is $5 — go get a new pair.'"
— Tom Lee, grandson
Even with a little money, Lee Yick Poon took care of everyone. 💜

A true story from our time...
💌
A cousin came home!
Uncle George had a grown-up son, George Jr., who lived far away for almost 50 years.
In November 2020, the family sent him a letter ✉️

He wrote back: he remembered Dad's grocery store in Alameda on Park Avenue — and weekends at the shop!

In July 2023 they met in person again. He drives a city bus in San Francisco (the busy 38 Geary line!) 🚌

Uncle George would be so proud: after all this time, George Jr., Gordon, Gilbert, and the rest of the family found each other again. 💛
Fun fact: George Jr., Gordon, and Gilbert all start with the letter G — like a family pattern!
